# Contributing to Common Parts Access

Thank you for your interest in Common Parts Access.

This document explains how contributions work and what is expected.

---

## Code contributions

**The codebase is not open to external contributions at this stage.**

Common Parts Access is maintained by the core team. The source code is public because transparency is foundational to what Common Parts is — anyone can read, verify, and understand how the platform works. For an infrastructure project built on trust, open source is not optional.

If you identify a security vulnerability, please contact us at [contact@commonparts.org](mailto:contact@commonparts.org) rather than opening a public issue.

---

## How to contribute

There are two meaningful ways to contribute to Common Parts:

### 1. Publish a spare part

The most direct contribution is adding a part to the registry.

Go to [access.commonparts.org/upload](https://access.commonparts.org/upload) and fill in the form. You can publish:

- **An original model** — a part you designed yourself. You retain authorship. The form collects the 3D files, images, metadata (compatible products, print settings, material), and license.

- **A curated model** — a part you found on an external platform (Printables, Thingiverse, GitHub, etc.) that is published under an open license allowing redistribution. The form includes a dedicated attribution section: source URL, original author name and profile link, source license, and original publication date.

**Quality expectations**

Whether original or curated, a published part should meet the following criteria:

- The part is identifiable: brand, product name, and part function are clearly described.
- The 3D file is printable. Prefer formats in this order: 3MF, STL, STEP.
- Print settings are provided where known (material, layer height, infill, supports).
- The license is correctly identified and compatible with redistribution.
- For curated parts: attribution is complete and accurate. Source URL, author name, and source license are all required fields.

Parts that do not meet these criteria may be flagged for review or removed from the registry.

---

### 2. Report an issue

If you find a bug in the application, an attribution error, a broken link, or a part that has been incorrectly categorized, use the feedback button at the bottom right of the app.

Issues are triaged automatically and reviewed by the core team.

---

## What Common Parts does not accept

- Pull requests modifying the application source code
- Issues requesting features (use the in-app feedback widget instead)
- Part submissions that do not respect the original license of the source model
- Parts with incomplete or fabricated attribution

---

## License reminder

By publishing a part on Common Parts Access, you confirm that:

- You are the author of the model, or
- The model is published under a license that permits redistribution with attribution, and you have provided complete and accurate attribution.

Common Parts does not claim ownership of models it does not author. All published parts retain their original license. Attribution to original creators is mandatory and displayed publicly.
